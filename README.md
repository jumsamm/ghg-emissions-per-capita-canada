# 2023: Tonnes of GHG Emissions per Person Across Canadian Provinces
This interactive choropleth map visualizes greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions in tonnes per person across Canadian provinces for 2023. The map is designed to highlight important regional differences using GIS, with darker shades indicating higher per-capita emissions. Alberta and Saskatchewan appear to have elevated per-capita emissions due to production of large amounts of oil, gas and electricity from fossil fuels and agricultural products. These two provinces also have relatively small populations compared to a neighboring province like Quebec, which amplifies per-capita values. The combination of energy-intensive industries and lower population density makes Alberta and Saskatchewan standout on the per-capita GHG emissions map.
